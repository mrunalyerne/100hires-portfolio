**Topic:** Why AI formatting advice doesn't work for all audiences

---

You asked Claude how to format your LinkedIn posts. It told you: > short lines > mobile optimised > one idea per line. You followed the advice. Posted it and then...ah. Same ol shiddy engagement as before. Here's the big problem I have with this: AI learned to write LinkedIn posts by studying what went viral across every audience and every niche and every industry. It has no idea who your readers are. So it gave you a playbook built for someone else's followers. Short line formatting works for broad, general audiences. If you're writing for a more sophisticated, more niche audience who want to be challenged by what they read, that style just irritates them. They want full paragraphs. They want substance. > walls of text aren't universally bad > short lines aren't universally good. The only question that matters: "Does this work for my audience?" Before you copy a format / viral post / Claude recommendation, ask that first.
