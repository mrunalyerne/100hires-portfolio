**Topic:** AI content lacks taste — someone with taste must be in the room

---

Every piece of AI-generated content says the same thing: "I expect your time. But you're not entitled to mine." You believe you're entitled to somebody's time and attention to consume a LinkedIn post, a newsletter, or a blog. But you couldn't be bothered to spend 20 minutes writing it yourself. This communicates one thing: My time is precious. Yours isn't. And your customers feel it. Even if they can't name it. AI doesn't have taste. That's your job, friend. Call me the Rick Rubin of AI content if you want, but somebody with some taste has to be in the room. Somebody who can hear the output and go: "No. That's not it. Try again." Somebody who knows what good feels like — even when they can't explain why. Without that person, you're just generating slop at scale. And slop doesn't convert. Nobody has ever bought something because a company overwhelmed them with mediocre marketing.
