**Topic:** LinkedIn Interest Graph and feed control

---

Do you find yourself in a content bubble lately? Your feed showing you the same topics, the same faces, the same takes — on repeat? I was there too. And I spent weeks thinking it was just LinkedIn doing LinkedIn things. But It wasn't. It was me and it's probably also you. Our LinkedIn feed is no longer a reflection of our network. It's a reflection of our behaviour. Every like. Every scroll-past. Every time you ghost a post after 3 seconds — that's a signal. And LinkedIn's Interest Graph is building a profile of you based entirely on what you do, not what you intend. So what's happening now? Most people are accidentally training their feed to show them mediocre content — because they engage with mediocre content. Here are 5 ways to start taking back control: 1. Comment with substance, not courtesy 2. Use saves intentionally 3. Build consistent engagement clusters 4. Switch between Top and Recent with purpose 5. Clean your feed regularly. Check the carousel for the ins and outs of each one. Ways 6 to 10 are inside the Algorithm Insights Report 2026 — dropping April 29th, built on 1.3 million posts and 6 months of data.
